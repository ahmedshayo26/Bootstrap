Daughters of September

i remember a time when the darkness used to haunt us. when ghosts would turn the whites in our eyes with midnight blurs, and everythin that lived behind our eyelids became risen nightmares, brought forth from the graves of fear to terrorise us for forgetting their dreams.

but now we have sunlight in our hearts, gathered gently into sunsets by our smiles. where rivers ran amok our faces life grew, dew rushed to settle where we felt green all over, and anything with wings came to warm themselves beneath our eyes now. flowers of color have began to bud in our iris, and you can tell from the way the butterflies move that they have been fantasizing about the nectar inside. our souls have settled like star dust in our bones. and these hands, these porcelain spaces where we offered prayer with the sorrow in our mouths came back with God's blessing, and we have been content ever since.


Her Name

Her name was pronounced like the stars, A luminous spell Spoken often By all sorts of dark poetry, And one of the first commands that God gave The unborn night. A universe was born after that, A big bang That brought colour to the glass of my eyes Every time The gaze from her pupils collided with mine, Spectrums of red came on the shores of her lips, Gentle waves of a sun kiss... I found blue whenever she was missing in my skies And yellow prints the sun left on her every morning it broke dawn To shake her eyelids awake... The earth beneath her feet... Her smile could draw silver constellations for the Petal wings On a dozen butterflies swarming inside my stomach Whenever I am around her. And when she held my hand the first time It was not air that left my lungs, It was the colour of life Brushing on my tongue so I painted her name Every time My heart longed to call upon her.
